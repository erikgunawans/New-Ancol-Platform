# **Executive Summary**

|  | The Opportunity PT Pembangunan Jaya Ancol Tbk (PJAA), a BUMD entity in the Recreation, Tourism & Entertainment sector with 501–2,000 employees, is actively seeking an AI-powered Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM) and Regulatory Compliance platform. The organization manages 201–500 active contracts, processes 50–500 legal documents monthly, and has received regulatory compliance findings within the last 12 months — creating urgency for a systematic compliance and contract management solution. This synthesis distills 53 structured questionnaire responses across 7 organizational roles (Director/C-Level, CIO/IT Director, Legal Counsel, Compliance Officer, Contract Manager, Internal Auditor, and Business Development) into actionable findings that should directly shape product strategy, module prioritization, and go-to-market positioning. |

# **Research Overview**

## **Methodology**

A structured multi-stakeholder questionnaire comprising 54 questions was administered to representatives of PT Pembangunan Jaya Ancol Tbk. The questionnaire covered 9 domains: Organization Profile, Document Volume & Types, Current Workflow & Pain Points, Module Priorities, Regulatory & Compliance Readiness, Technical Infrastructure, User Roles & Access Control, Integration & Notifications, AI Quality & Human Review Preferences, Budget & Timeline, Security & Data Governance, and Training & Change Management. 53 of 54 questions received responses (98.1% completion rate).

# **Key Findings**

Seven major findings emerged from the thematic analysis, ordered by the combined weight of frequency (how many domains corroborate the finding) and impact (severity of business consequence if left unaddressed).

| Finding 1: Active Regulatory Exposure Demands Immediate Compliance Automation |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** PJAA received compliance findings or regulatory sanctions within the last 12 months (PJAA-24). Simultaneously, the organization has not yet appointed a Data Protection Officer as required by UU PDP No. 27/2022 (PJAA-20), has no documented data inventory or register (PJAA-21), and relies entirely on external legal consultants to flag regulatory changes (PJAA-22). The organization prioritized the Regulatory Compliance & Audit Engine as the first module to implement (PJAA-16). **Frequency:** 7/9 domains   |   **Impact:** Critical   |   **Confidence:** High |

| Finding 2: Contract Obligation Visibility Is the Highest-Risk Operational Gap |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** With 201–500 active contracts requiring ongoing obligation monitoring (PJAA-10), PJAA identified missed contract renewal and obligation deadlines as a top-3 critical pain point (PJAA-12). The most critical obligation types to track include auto-renewal opt-out periods, reporting obligations, and termination triggers (PJAA-41). No centralized system exists for obligation tracking — audit findings are managed in spreadsheets (PJAA-14). **Frequency:** 5/9 domains   |   **Impact:** Critical   |   **Confidence:** High |

| Finding 3: Drafting Inconsistency Creates Systemic Legal Risk |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** Templates exist but are applied inconsistently across teams (PJAA-11). First draft turnaround exceeds 2 business days (PJAA-13). Drafting standards inconsistency was flagged as a critical pain point (PJAA-12). This spans 7 document types across multiple departments — from NDAs and vendor contracts to land/property leases and Board resolutions (PJAA-07). The top-priority Module 2 features were the Smart Drafting Workbench, Contract Risk Scanner, and Conversational Document Q\&A (PJAA-17). **Frequency:** 4/9 domains   |   **Impact:** High   |   **Confidence:** High |

| Finding 4: Institutional Knowledge Loss Is an Unaddressed Strategic Risk |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** Siloed institutional knowledge — where expertise is lost when staff leave — was identified as one of seven critical pain points (PJAA-12). Combined with physical documents that cannot be digitally processed (also flagged in PJAA-12), the organization lacks a durable knowledge layer. The Conversational Document Q\&A feature (ask any contract a natural-language question) was selected as a top-3 priority (PJAA-17), signaling appetite for a self-service knowledge retrieval system. **Frequency:** 3/9 domains   |   **Impact:** High   |   **Confidence:** Medium |

| Finding 5: Mobile-First Executive Workflow Is Non-Negotiable |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** The Mobile Approver Interface was rated “Critical” — Directors and C-Level executives frequently need to approve decisions without being at their desks (PJAA-18). WhatsApp is widely used for cross-team business communication (PJAA-34), and the organization wants WhatsApp notifications for audit task assignments, status updates, and obligation deadline reminders (PJAA-35). This is not a nice-to-have; it is a deployment blocker for executive adoption. **Frequency:** 4/9 domains   |   **Impact:** Critical   |   **Confidence:** High |

| Finding 6: Digital Security Posture Has Foundational Gaps |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** PJAA does not use Multi-Factor Authentication (PJAA-32). IT offboarding is informal and not documented (PJAA-33). Security audits are internal-only with no third-party penetration testing (PJAA-46). The incident response plan exists on paper but has never been tested (PJAA-48). These gaps are material for a platform that will process sensitive legal documents and personal data subject to UU PDP compliance. **Frequency:** 3/9 domains   |   **Impact:** High   |   **Confidence:** High |

| Finding 7: AI Trust Requires Transparent Confidence Scoring and Bahasa Indonesia Fluency |
| :---- |
| **Evidence:** The top organizational concern about AI adoption is hallucination — generating legally incorrect decisions or advice (PJAA-53). The organization requires threshold-based Human-in-the-Loop oversight at \>85% AI confidence before accepting automated compliance decisions (PJAA-38, PJAA-39). Bahasa Indonesia quality in AI outputs is rated “Important” (PJAA-40). Despite this caution, PJAA self-identifies as an early adopter actively seeking AI tools to transform legal operations (PJAA-50). **Frequency:** 4/9 domains   |   **Impact:** High   |   **Confidence:** High |

# **Opportunity Areas**

Based on the seven findings and four personas, the following opportunity areas represent the highest-impact product investments for the PJAA engagement and the broader Indonesian regulated-entity market.

## **Opportunity 1: Proactive Regulatory Intelligence Engine**

|  | Unmet Need PJAA currently has zero internal capability for systematic regulatory change tracking. They rely entirely on external legal consultants to flag changes across 7+ regulatory sources (JDIH, IDX, Perda DKI, ministerial regulations, BKPM, PUPR, tourism). The recent compliance findings/sanctions (within 12 months) prove this approach is failing. |
| :---- | :---- |

**Product Implication:** Build an automated regulatory crawler that monitors JDIH, IDX announcements, and DKI Jakarta’s Perda database, matches changes against PJAA’s active contract clauses and obligation register, and generates impact assessments with a confidence score. This directly replaces the external consultant dependency and becomes the platform’s strongest differentiator for Indonesian BUMD/BUMN entities.

**Addressable Market Signal:** Every publicly listed BUMD faces identical multi-source regulatory tracking challenges. This feature alone could anchor the value proposition for the entire Indonesian SOE market segment.

## **Opportunity 2: Obligation Lifecycle Tracker with Predictive Alerting**

|  | Unmet Need 201–500 active contracts with no centralized obligation register. Renewal deadlines, reporting frequencies, and termination trigger dates are tracked manually or in disconnected spreadsheets. Missed deadlines are flagged as a critical organizational pain point. |
| :---- | :---- |

**Product Implication:** Develop an obligation extraction engine that parses executed contracts to identify renewal dates, opt-out windows, reporting cadences, and termination notice periods. Layer predictive alerting (30/14/7-day cascading reminders) with WhatsApp delivery for field-accessible notifications. The obligation dashboard becomes the Contract Steward’s primary daily interface.

## **Opportunity 3: Intelligent Drafting Workbench with Clause-Level Risk Scoring**

|  | Unmet Need Templates exist but are inconsistently applied across departments. Seven distinct document types (NDAs, vendor contracts, sale-purchase agreements, JV agreements, land/property leases, employment contracts, SOPs/board resolutions) need standardized, risk-aware drafting workflows. First draft turnaround exceeds 2 business days. |
| :---- | :---- |

**Product Implication:** Build a structured-form drafting interface with pre-approved clause libraries per document type, real-time risk flagging (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW per clause), and bilingual output (Bahasa Indonesia primary, English secondary). Target \<4 hours for a standardized first draft. The Conversational Q\&A layer allows any stakeholder to interrogate contracts in natural language.

## **Opportunity 4: Mobile-Native Executive Approval Layer**

|  | Unmet Need Directors and C-Level executives are frequently away from their desks and cannot participate in HITL approval flows or review compliance decisions. WhatsApp is already the dominant business communication channel. Mobile approval was rated “Critical” — not optional. |
| :---- | :---- |

**Product Implication:** Design a mobile-first approval interface (progressive web app or native) with WhatsApp deep-link notifications. Approvers should be able to review AI confidence scores, see risk summaries, and approve/reject with one tap. This is a deployment prerequisite, not a Phase 2 feature.

## **Opportunity 5: Security Hardening Advisory as Platform Value-Add**

|  | Unmet Need No MFA, informal offboarding, internal-only security audits, and an untested incident response plan. The platform will process sensitive legal and personal data subject to UU PDP. PJAA’s current security posture creates risk for both the client and the platform provider. |
| :---- | :---- |

**Product Implication:** Bundle a Security Readiness Assessment as part of onboarding. The platform should enforce MFA at the application layer (Google Workspace SSO with mandatory 2FA), implement automated session revocation for offboarded users, and provide a UU PDP compliance starter kit (DPO appointment guidance, data inventory template, breach response playbook). This positions the product as a governance upgrade, not just a legal tool.

# **Recommendations**

## **Phase 1 (Weeks 1–8): Foundation**

•  Deploy Module 1 (Regulatory Compliance & Audit Engine) as the entry point — this is the client’s explicit first priority and addresses their most acute pain (recent regulatory findings). Configure crawlers for JDIH, IDX, Perda DKI, and relevant ministerial regulation sources.  
•  Implement Mobile Approver Interface from Day 1 — not as a later enhancement. Without mobile approval, executive adoption will stall and HITL workflows will bottleneck.  
•  Bundle Security Hardening: enforce MFA via Google Workspace SSO integration, implement automated access revocation, and deliver a UU PDP Readiness Starter Kit (DPO nomination guide, data inventory template).  
•  Integrate with Dokmee DMS via REST API for document ingestion and with Google Workspace for export/collaboration. Keep the integration scope narrow — PJAA confirmed no additional third-party integrations are needed.

## **Phase 2 (Weeks 9–16): Contract Lifecycle**

•  Deploy Module 2 (Legal Document Automation) with the Smart Drafting Workbench, Contract Risk Scanner, and Conversational Q\&A as the top-3 features.  
•  Launch the Obligation Lifecycle Tracker: auto-extract renewal dates, reporting cadences, and termination triggers from the existing 201–500 contract corpus. Activate WhatsApp-based cascading deadline reminders.  
•  Deliver bilingual training via on-demand video tutorials (Bahasa Indonesia \+ English) and in-app guided tours — the two formats PJAA identified as most effective.

## **Phase 3 (Months 5–6): Intelligence & Scale**

•  Activate point-in-time compliance querying (“Was this contract compliant as of \[date\]?”) for internal audit investigations.  
•  Deploy the Conversational Document Q\&A layer across the full contract repository to address institutional knowledge loss.  
•  Produce the formal ROI/Business Case deck for board presentation, incorporating actual usage metrics, time-saved calculations, and compliance risk reduction data from Phases 1–2.

## **Go-to-Market Implications**

PJAA’s profile — a publicly listed BUMD managing complex multi-source regulatory obligations with a hybrid infrastructure and Google Workspace ecosystem — is representative of the broader Indonesian SOE market. Product decisions validated with PJAA should be designed for reuse:

* **Regulatory source coverage:** JDIH \+ IDX \+ Perda DKI is the minimum viable set for listed BUMDs. Sektoral (BKPM, PUPR, tourism) adds differentiation.  
* **Bahasa Indonesia AI quality:** This is not a localization feature — it is a market-entry requirement for every Indonesian enterprise customer.  
* **WhatsApp integration:** WhatsApp Business is the dominant internal communication channel in Indonesian enterprises. Notification delivery via WhatsApp is a competitive necessity.  
* **Data residency (asia-southeast2):** Hard requirement for all Indonesian government-linked entities processing legal and personal data.
